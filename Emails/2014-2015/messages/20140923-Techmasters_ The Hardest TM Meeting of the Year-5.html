What is hardware? You might ask.

hard·ware, noun, \ˈhärd-ˌwer\
1. The components of a computer only repairable through the use of ample kinetic energy, such as that delivered by a kicking or punching strike to the computer


In all seriousness, hardware consists of all the physical components making up a computer (which all Techmasters involved in work duty must remember we are not allowed to service or repair!). Hardware differs from software in that software is completely virtual, with the only physical manifestation being the 1's and 0's (positive and negative charges) stored on the computer.
