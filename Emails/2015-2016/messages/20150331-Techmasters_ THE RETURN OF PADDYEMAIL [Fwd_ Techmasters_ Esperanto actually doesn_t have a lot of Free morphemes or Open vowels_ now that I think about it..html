Can anybody really own a language?  Does anyone really own English?  Or Japanese?  Or French?  Certainly L’académie française think they do, but do they have any basis for that?  It's a weird thought when Paramount Pictures say they have the full rights to the Klingon language, including any works enterprising young Star Trek fans produce in it.


Fortunately (or maybe unfortunately?) it's never been challenged in court, so we don't really know.  I guess for now, you're Free to use it.


 


I do know one thing for sure, though: legally, someone can really own a piece of software.  Most of the software you use is probably owned by someone, and that someone probably isn't you.  This person might be really mean and not let you use the software how you want, or look and see how it was made.  They may remove videos of a video game not being played in the way they think it should be [1], or they may not let you (or the FDA!) see the source code to the pacemaker that's keeping you alive [2].  They may charge you money.  It may not be Free.  But there are other people out there who do the opposite.  They let you read the code.  They let you distribute the software.  They *want* you to distribute the software, even if you change it.  This new type of software will open doors!  Open lives!  Open Sesame!


 


Open Source, that is.
